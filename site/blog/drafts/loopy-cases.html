Customer Story

How Loopy Cases consolidated every asset into one platform

A phone accessory brand fully migrated from Box to Collage, building a photographer-first folder system with tag-driven discovery and a brand portal that serves every team.

Company Loopy Cases
Industry Consumer Goods
Team Size 2 primary users
Use Case Photography & Brand Management
The Customer

A creative lead managing high-volume product photography

Loopy Cases is a phone accessory brand known for its signature loop grip. Jamie Ross leads the creative asset workflow, coordinating photography from multiple external photographers and an in-house photographer, Mariah, who also handles all tagging and bulk uploads.

From product launches and campaign shoots to Photoshop mockups showing cases on every phone color, the team produces a constant stream of visual content that needs to reach graphic designers, marketing, and the founders.

The Challenge

Migrating from Box without losing structure

Before Collage, Loopy Cases managed all assets in Box using a deeply nested folder structure organized by photographer and shoot number. When they migrated, every photographer's carefully separated folders were dumped into a single location — losing the hierarchy the team depended on.

On top of the migration challenge, sharing assets meant juggling Box permissions across departments. Graphic designers, marketing, influencer relations, quality assurance, and even external charity partners all needed access to different slices of the library — but giving everyone full access created confusion and risk.

1

A photographer-first folder system that keeps every shoot organized

Jamie rebuilt the team's asset structure inside Collage using folders organized by photographer, then by numbered shoot. Kiana's 55th shoot gets its own folder. Mariah's in-house work is organized the same way. From each folder, selected images are shared into campaign-specific collages for marketing.

This hybrid approach gives the team the deep structure they need for managing thousands of images, while collages serve as curated sets for each launch — final photos, ads, working files, and giveaway content, all in one place.

"I know you guys hate folders, but we use folders a lot. We have a lot of stuff that doesn't necessarily need a collage — the collage part of it is for product grouping."
Photographers / Kiana / Shoot 55
Barry Stripes Multi-Grasp Pocket Shot
2

Tag-driven retrieval powered by a dedicated tagging steward

Mariah manages all tagging for the library — product names, loop combos, seasonal themes like Valentine's Day, use cases like multi-grasp and pocket shots, and even specific elements in photos like jewelry, balloons, or sweaters.

The result is a system where anyone on the team can combine tags to find exactly what they need. Search "Barry Stripes" plus "multi-grasp" and the right images appear instantly — no folder-diving required.

"As long as we're keeping up with our tags, we find it very, very easy to find what we're looking for."
barry stripes + multi-grasp

3

A brand portal that serves every department and partner

Loopy Cases built a brand portal in Collage with organized folders for production, customer service, influencer relations, quality assurance, and the design team. Each folder contains exactly what that department needs — product images, color files, company logos, and external charity logos.

When the influencer team needs a preview of upcoming launches, Jamie shares a folder with expiration dates and login requirements for security. When a charity partner needs a logo, Jamie points them to the portal instead of hunting it down manually.

"Before it was always 'Hey, can you find this logo for me?' I'm like, no — check the brand portal."

4

A complete migration from Box — no backup plan, no going back

Jamie didn't just add Collage to the tool stack — she replaced Box entirely. After migrating all assets, she emptied Box completely so the founders had no choice but to adopt the new system. A "pre-collage" staging folder held legacy content that gets organized as time allows.

The result: zero confusion about where files live. Edited working files go in folders, final approved images go in collages. No Box, no Dropbox, no duplicate storage. Just Collage.

"We literally left Box empty. So then they couldn't — the founders had nothing to go to. We forced it upon them and it worked."

"It's less confusion as far as what is where. Edited files are going to be in a folder. If it's just an image that we know is final, you can use it for whatever — it's in a collage. It just makes it so easy to deliver to whatever team we need to deliver it to."
Jamie Ross, Loopy Cases
