Customer Story

How Parker Baby Co replaced folder-digging with tag-based discovery

A growing baby brand's marketing team uses Collage to organize thousands of assets, power seasonal campaigns, and distribute content daily — all from one platform.

Company Parker Baby Co
Industry Consumer Goods
Team Size 4 — 6 people
Use Case Marketing & Social
The Customer

A small team with thousands of assets

Parker Baby Co is a growing baby and kids brand with a lean but cross-functional marketing team. The team includes a marketing coordinator, a graphic designer, a paid ads contractor, and an Amazon specialist — all producing and consuming a high volume of visual content daily.

From lifestyle photography and studio shoots to UGC and social media creatives, the team generates thousands of assets every quarter across multiple product lines and campaigns.

The Challenge

Folders organized by product, not by need

Before Collage, Parker Baby's assets lived in Google Drive, organized rigidly by product. Finding content meant navigating deep folder hierarchies — clicking through specific bib sets, swaddles, and headbands to find the right image.

But when the team needed assets for a seasonal campaign like Valentine's Day, or wanted a photo of "a mom holding a baby" regardless of the product, Drive's product-first structure became a bottleneck. There was no way to search by concept, feel, or campaign theme. Sharing with external contractors meant managing Drive permissions. And the daily workflow of downloading that day's social content to a phone was clunky and slow.

1

Tag-based discovery replaced product-first searching

With Collage, Parker Baby built a tag-based organization system that captures the characteristics their team actually searches for — seasonal themes like Valentine's Day or winter, settings like outdoors, subjects like "mom holding baby," and content formats like story, feed, or pin.

Now, when the graphic designer needs assets for a Valentine's Day campaign, she doesn't dig through every product folder looking for red and pink imagery. She searches by tag and sees everything at a glance.

"Instead of digging around in every single product folder, anything that fits the general vibe of Valentine's, we would have tagged that way. She can just see all of those at a quick glance. That saves so much time."

2

Campaign collages keep creative output organized

Parker Baby's graphic designer, Jordi, is the team's heaviest Collage user. For each social media campaign, she creates the story asset, the feed post, and a Pinterest pin — then drops all of them into campaign-specific collages organized by brand, year, and quarter.

This structure means the marketing coordinator can always find the right creative for any campaign at a glance, and assets are easy to reference or repurpose in the future.

"Each social idea that I'm bringing her, Jordi creates the story asset, the feed, and then a Pinterest pin, and drops them all in here."

3

Frictionless sharing with contractors and models

Parker Baby works with external contractors for paid ads and partners with models for photo shoots. Before Collage, sharing meant managing Drive permissions and worrying about access issues.

Now, shared collages give contractors and partners direct access to exactly the assets they need. The team even shares model-specific collages so models like Troy can access their own photos for their personal portfolios.

"She can also go in these and not have to worry about access issues."

4

A daily mobile workflow powered by collage links

Every morning, the marketing coordinator opens the team's content calendar, clicks a Collage link, and downloads that day's stories and pins directly to her phone. It's a simple but essential daily ritual that keeps content flowing across Instagram, Pinterest, and the brand's social channels.

By linking each calendar entry directly to a Collage, the team eliminated the need to search for the right assets every morning — everything is one tap away.

"Every morning I start on our content calendar. I always link the collage so that the next day, I can easily get those stories and those pins."
